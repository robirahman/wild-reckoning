Remaining feedback from the game designer's playtesting (items already addressed have been removed)

- Territory map is inexplicable, and clicking on dots doesn't seem to work half the time.  I just saw a floating nose and dots and clicked them until I ran out of energy.  What does it mean that my current location is WATER?  What does Perception: 1 mean?  Why is there a regional ecosystem meter why am I supposed to care about that?  So I chose to run across the road and got killed by a car.
- the +TRA, +ADV, etc. notifiers should, when moused over, highlight the corresponding stat bar and make the explanation appear.  The explanation text should appear in some sort of fixed infobox below all the bars.  I would make sure the descriptions were custom written to be good, since they're important for the player.  Something like,
  **ADV**ersity tracks the level of hardship you're facing in your environment.  Things like overpopulation and old age make life harder for you; shelter and a sense of security make it easier.
  "**TRA**uma, or post-traumatic stress, is a consequence of, and mental adaptation to, exposure to extreme situations like predator encounters and starvation.  These leave marks on your mind that never go away.
**NOV**elty represents exposure to new and unfamiliar situations that you haven't adapted to, or where you lack evolved instincts to guide you."  And so on.
- Ten turns in and I've already got like twenty adversity debuffs, mostly from individual situations that passed or from +5 age-phase.  Also why am I constantly losing -5 age-phase from wisdom?  Stats are really unbalanced and hard to manage tactically; I'd change the balance dramatically.
- Maybe it should specify that I can only choose one of the actions at the bottom per turn, e.g. hovering over one decreases the opacity of the others, and when clicked, it remains bolded while the others get crossed out or remain translucent.  Too easy to forget to do any of them.
- The debriefing doesn't work if everything is just plainly stated in game
